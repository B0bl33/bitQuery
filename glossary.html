Address

A unique string of characters that represents a destination for cryptocurrency transactions. It is derived from the public key.

Airdrop

The distribution of free tokens to a cryptocurrency community, often as a marketing strategy or reward for early supporters.

Altcoin

Any cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin. Altcoins include a wide range of projects with different use cases and technologies.

Blockchain

A decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across a network of computers. It is the underlying technology behind cryptocurrencies.

Burning

The process of permanently removing a certain amount of cryptocurrency from circulation, typically to reduce supply and increase value.

Centralized

A system or organization where control and decision-making are concentrated in a single authority or a small group of individuals.

Cold Storage

The practice of keeping cryptocurrency offline to protect it from hacking and cyber threats. Cold storage methods include hardware wallets and paper wallets.

Consensus Mechanism

The method by which a blockchain network agrees on the validity of transactions. Common mechanisms include Proof of Work (PoW) and Proof of Stake (PoS).

DAO

n organization governed by smart contracts and blockchain technology, with decision-making power distributed among its members.

DApp

An application that runs on a blockchain network, utilizing smart contracts for its functionality. DApps are open-source and operate without a central authority.

Decentralization

The distribution of power and control away from a central authority, allowing a network of nodes to manage and validate transactions collectively.

DeFi

Decentralized Financial services and applications built on blockchain technology that operate without intermediaries like banks. DeFi includes lending, borrowing, trading, and more.

DEX

Decentralized Exchanges. A type of cryptocurrency exchange that operates without a central authority, allowing users to trade directly with one another through smart contracts.

DYOR

Do Your Own Research. A reminder to investors to thoroughly research and understand a project before investing in it, rather than relying solely on external opinions.

FOMO

Fear Of Missing Out. The anxiety that an investor feels when they believe they might miss out on a potentially profitable investment opportunity.

Fork

A split in a blockchain network that results in two separate chains. Forks can be either soft (backward-compatible) or hard (non-backward-compatible).

Gas

A unit of measurement for the computational effort required to execute transactions and smart contracts on the Ethereum blockchain. Gas fees are paid to miners for their work.

Gas Limit

The maximum amount of gas a user is willing to spend on a transaction or smart contract execution on the Ethereum network.

Hash

A fixed-length alphanumeric string generated by a cryptographic algorithm. Hashes are used to represent data uniquely and ensure the integrity of transactions.

HODL

A slang term in the cryptocurrency community that stands for "Hold On for Dear Life." It refers to the strategy of holding onto a cryptocurrency investment for the long term, regardless of market volatility.

Hot Wallet

A cryptocurrency wallet that is connected to the internet, allowing for quick access to funds but also exposing it to potential security risks.

ICO Initial Coin Offering

A fundraising method where new cryptocurrency projects sell tokens to early investors in exchange for capital, similar to an IPO in the stock market.

KYC Know Your Customer

A regulatory process that requires financial institutions and cryptocurrency exchanges to verify the identity of their customers to prevent fraud and money laundering.

Layer 2

A secondary framework or protocol built on top of an existing blockchain (Layer 1) to improve scalability and reduce transaction costs.

Liquidity

The ease with which an asset can be bought or sold in the market without affecting its price. High liquidity indicates a large number of buyers and sellers, leading to more stable prices.

Mining

The process of validating and recording transactions on a blockchain by solving complex mathematical problems. Miners are rewarded with newly created cryptocurrency for their efforts.

Multi-Signature

A security feature that requires multiple private keys to authorize a transaction, enhancing the security of cryptocurrency storage.

NFT Non Fungible Token

A unique digital asset that represents ownership of a specific item or piece of content, such as art, music, or virtual real estate. NFTs are indivisible and cannot be exchanged on a one-to-one basis like cryptocurrencies.

NGMI

Short for "Not Gonna Make It." A term used to express doubt or negativity about the success of a project or individual's actions within the crypto space.

Nodes

Individual computers or devices that participate in a blockchain network by validating and relaying transactions. Nodes help maintain the integrity and security of the blockchain.

Oracle

A service that provides external data to smart contracts, enabling them to interact with real-world events and information.

Private Key

A secret code that allows the owner to access and manage their cryptocurrency. It should be kept secure and not shared with anyone.

Proof Of Stake PoS

A consensus mechanism where validators are chosen to create new blocks and validate transactions based on the number of coins they hold and are willing to "stake" as collateral.

Proof Of Work

A consensus mechanism where miners solve complex mathematical puzzles to validate transactions and add them to the blockchain. It requires significant computational power.

Public Key

A cryptographic code that allows users to receive cryptocurrency. It can be shared publicly without compromising security.

Roadmap

A strategic plan that outlines the milestones and goals of a cryptocurrency project over time. It provides a timeline for development, features, and future updates.

Rug Pull

A type of scam where developers abandon a project and run away with investors' funds, leaving the token worthless.

Sharding

A scaling solution that divides a blockchain network into smaller, more manageable pieces (shards) to increase transaction throughput and reduce latency.

Smart Contract

Self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement directly written into code. They automatically enforce and execute the terms when conditions are met.

Stablecoin

A type of cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value by being pegged to a reserve asset like a fiat currency (e.g., USD) or a commodity (e.g., gold).

Staking

The act of participating in a Proof of Stake (PoS) network by locking up a certain amount of cryptocurrency to support network operations and earn rewards.

Token

A digital asset created on a blockchain, representing ownership or a utility in a project. Tokens can be used for various purposes, such as accessing services or voting on decisions.

Tokenomics

The economic model and design of a cryptocurrency or token, including its supply, distribution, and incentive mechanisms.

Wallet

A digital tool that allows users to store, manage, and interact with their cryptocurrencies. Wallets can be hardware-based, software-based, or online (web wallets).

WAGMI

Short for "Weâ€™re All Gonna Make It." A term used to express optimism and confidence in the success of a project or the crypto community at large.

Whale

A term used to describe an individual or entity that holds a large amount of cryptocurrency, often capable of influencing market prices.

Whitepaper

A detailed document released by a cryptocurrency project that outlines the technology, purpose, and roadmap of the project. It serves as an authoritative guide to the project's vision and implementation.

Yield Farming

A DeFi activity where users lend or stake their cryptocurrency to earn interest or other rewards, often involving complex strategies to maximize returns.
